Theory & Notes

• A prime is an integer greater than 1 with no positive divisors other than 1 and itself.
• Fundamental Theorem of Arithmetic: Every integer n ≥ 2 factors uniquely as a product of primes (up to order).
• Trial division: test divisibility by 2, then odd numbers up to √n.
• Next/Previous prime: check successive odd numbers; probabilistic tests (e.g., Miller–Rabin) scale better for huge n.
• Factoring tips: remove small primes early; for large n use Pollard's Rho, ECM, or libraries.
