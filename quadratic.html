Theory & Notes

• A quadratic is a polynomial of degree 2: f(x)=ax²+bx+c with a≠0.
• Discriminant Δ=b²−4ac determines roots: Δ>0 two reals, Δ=0 one real double, Δ<0 complex conjugates.
• Roots formula: x = (−b ± √Δ) / (2a). Vertex at x=−b/(2a), y=f(x).
• Graph: parabola opening up if a>0, down if a<0. Axis of symmetry through the vertex x=−b/(2a).
• Completing the square: ax²+bx+c = a[(x+ b/(2a))² − Δ/(4a²)] gives vertex form.
