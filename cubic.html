Theory & Notes

• A cubic is degree 3: f(x)=ax³+bx²+cx+d with a≠0.
• Roots count: at least one real root; depending on discriminant, either one real + two complex, or three real roots.
• Depressed cubic: substitute x=t−b/(3a) to get t³+pt+q=0. Solve via Cardano or trigonometric formulas.
• Shape: S-like if three real roots; monotonic if one real root. Inflection at f''(x)=0 → x=−b/(3a).
• Numerical practice: for general inputs prefer robust library solvers; symbolic formulas can be ill-conditioned.
