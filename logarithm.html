Theory & Notes

• Definition: For b > 0, b ≠ 1 and x > 0, logb(x) is the exponent y such that by = x.
• Change of base: logb(x) = ln(x)/ln(b) = log10(x)/log10(b).
• Laws: log(xy)=log x + log y; log(x/y)=log x − log y; log(xk)=k·log x.
• Shapes: For b>1, logb(x) increases slowly; for 00, range is all real numbers.
• Typical bases: e (natural log), 10 (common log), 2 (binary log).
